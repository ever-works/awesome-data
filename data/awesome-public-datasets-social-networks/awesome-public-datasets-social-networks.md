## Overview
Awesome Public Datasets – Social Networks is a curated, themed subset of the broader Awesome Public Datasets project. It focuses specifically on high-quality, publicly available social network datasets (such as Twitter scrapes, Enron email data, and social graphs from platforms like Facebook), organized in an "awesome-style" directory format.

Each dataset entry is accompanied by structured metadata files to make discovery, comparison, and reuse easier for researchers, developers, and data practitioners.

---

## Features

- **Themed directory for social network data**  
  Focuses exclusively on datasets related to social networks and communication graphs (e.g., Twitter, email networks, social graphs).

- **Curated subset of Awesome Public Datasets**  
  Drawn from the larger Awesome Public Datasets ecosystem, ensuring a baseline of quality and relevance.

- **Structured metadata for each dataset**  
  Each dataset entry includes metadata files in a consistent format, simplifying:
  - Discovery and search
  - Comparison across datasets
  - Integration with other tools in the awesome ecosystem

- **Covers multiple social platforms and network types**  
  Examples mentioned include:
  - Twitter scrapes
  - Enron email corpus
  - Facebook-like social graphs

- **Ecosystem-friendly design**  
  Organized to work well with other awesome-style lists and tooling, enabling reuse and cross-linking within the broader awesome data ecosystem.

- **Open and publicly accessible**  
  Catalogs high-quality datasets that are publicly available, suitable for research, experimentation, and educational purposes (subject to each dataset’s own license/terms).

---

## Use Cases

- Finding social network datasets for academic research or publications
- Prototyping graph algorithms, network analysis, and recommendation systems
- Building tutorials or educational materials on social network analysis
- Benchmarking tools for graph databases, community detection, or influence modeling

---

## Pricing

- Not a commercial product; the directory itself is freely accessible on GitHub.  
- Individual datasets may have their own licenses or usage restrictions, which need to be checked per dataset.

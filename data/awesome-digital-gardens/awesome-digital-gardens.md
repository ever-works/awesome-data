## Overview

Awesome Digital Gardens curates inspirational digital gardens and provides resources for creating personal knowledge management systems, featuring established gardens, guides, tools, and complementary projects.

## Features

- **Curated Examples**: Notable digital gardens including gwern.net, Andy Matuschak's Working Notes, and Maggie Appleton's Garden showcasing diverse approaches to organizing personal knowledge
- **Educational Resources**: Foundational guides like Tom Critchlow's "Building a digital garden" and Joel Hooks' introduction exploring the concept
- **Tool Ecosystem**: Multiple software options from established platforms like Dendron and are.na to emerging solutions like _Prtcl and digital-garden (Rust-based content authoring)
- **Thematic Categories**: Favorite gardens, creation guides, active gardening tools, beta-stage applications, and complementary projects
- **Philosophy Exploration**: Projects exploring knowledge organization philosophy, notation systems, and personal belief documentation

## Use Cases

- Building personal wikis and knowledge bases
- Creating public learning notes and documentation
- Organizing research and ideas
- Sharing evolving thoughts and work-in-progress content
- Implementing Zettelkasten or second brain methodologies

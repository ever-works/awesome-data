# Awesome SaltStack

A collaboratively curated, open-source directory of high‑quality Salt/SaltStack learning materials, tools, and ecosystem resources.

- **Category:** Themed Directories
- **Tags:** DevOps, Configuration, Awesome Lists
- **Source:** <https://github.com/hbokh/awesome-saltstack#readme>

---

## Description

Awesome SaltStack is an “awesome list” style collection focused on Salt (SaltStack), the Python-based configuration management and infrastructure automation system. It aggregates links to tutorials, official docs, community tools, integrations, and reference materials relevant to SaltStack users, from beginners to advanced operators.

---

## Features

- **Curated Awesome List**  
  A maintained list of vetted SaltStack-related resources, following the general "awesome" list conventions.

- **Focus on Salt/SaltStack**  
  Dedicated to Salt, the Python-based configuration management and remote execution framework used by DevOps teams, system administrators, and web‑scale application developers.

- **Organized Contents**  
  The repository groups resources into multiple sections (as headings in the README):
  - **Official resources** – Links to official Salt/SaltStack project sites and primary documentation.
  - **Tutorials** – Guides and how‑tos for learning Salt from scratch or deepening existing knowledge.
  - **Code** – Example code, modules, and related repositories relevant to SaltStack usage and development.
  - **Integrations** – Resources showing how Salt integrates with other systems, tools, and platforms.
  - **Books** – Published books and long-form written references about Salt/SaltStack.
  - **Videos** – Talks, screencasts, and video tutorials.
  - **Tools** – Utilities and complementary software that enhance or extend SaltStack usage.
  - **Presentations** – Slide decks and conference/session materials.
  - **Blogposts and opinions** – Articles and commentary discussing SaltStack usage, best practices, and experiences.
  - **Discussions** – Community discussion venues (for example, `/r/sysadmin` on Reddit and similar spaces).
  - **Community** – Community links and hubs around the Salt/SaltStack ecosystem.
  - **Formulas** – Collections of Salt formulas for managing specific applications or systems.
  - **Cheat sheets** – Quick reference materials for Salt commands and configuration.
  - **Uncategorized** – Items that don’t neatly fit into the other categories.
  - **Attic** – Older or less‑active resources of potential historical or reference interest.

- **Learning-Oriented**  
  Emphasis on tutorials, guides, and structured learning materials for users at different experience levels.

- **Ecosystem Coverage**  
  Includes not only documentation but also tools, community projects, blog posts, and opinions that reflect real‑world Salt usage.

- **Open Source & Community Contributions**  
  Hosted on GitHub with a `CONTRIBUTING.md`, allowing the community to propose additions and improvements.

- **Licensed Content**  
  The repository includes a `LICENSE` file defining terms for use and contribution.

- **Context on SaltStack**  
  Provides a brief overview of Salt/SaltStack, its performance goals (speed, scalability), and its common use cases in managing large-scale server fleets and application stacks. Notes that SaltStack was acquired by VMware in October 2020.

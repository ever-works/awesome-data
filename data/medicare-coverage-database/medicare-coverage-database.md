## Overview
The Medicare Coverage Database is a U.S. federal resource maintained by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). It provides structured, publicly accessible information on Medicare coverage decisions, and is referenced in healthcare data and policy contexts (including public datasets lists focused on health).

## Features
- **Centralized coverage information**  
  Aggregates official Medicare coverage information in one place for use by clinicians, plans, researchers, and policymakers.

- **National Coverage Determinations (NCDs)**  
  Includes structured documentation of national-level decisions on whether specific items and services are covered under Medicare.

- **Local Coverage Determinations (LCDs)**  
  Provides information on regionally administered coverage decisions made by Medicare Administrative Contractors, including variations by locality.

- **Structured, queryable data**  
  Organizes coverage information in a structured format suitable for systematic review, policy analysis, and data-driven research.

- **Regulatory and policy alignment**  
  Reflects how Medicare benefits and medical-necessity standards are interpreted and applied at national and local levels, supporting analysis of MA (Medicare Advantage) and Traditional Medicare coverage alignment.

- **Research and dataset reference**  
  Cited in public healthcare dataset compilations, making it a recognized data source for health services research, utilization management analysis, and policy studies.

## Use Cases
- Analyzing how Medicare defines and applies coverage for specific services or technologies.  
- Comparing national versus local coverage rules across regions.  
- Supporting compliance, utilization management, and physician advisor work related to Medicare coverage.  
- Incorporating structured coverage rules into broader healthcare datasets and policy research.

## Overview

Awesome Theravada is a curated "Awesome"-style directory of resources, texts, and materials related to the Theravada Buddhist tradition, which draws its doctrine from the Pāli Canon, one of the oldest surviving records of the Buddha's teachings.

## Features

- **Curated Awesome List**
  - Organized collection of Theravada Buddhist teachings and related materials.
  - Follows the common "Awesome" list format hosted on GitHub.

- **Topical Structure**
  - **Academic** – scholarly and academic resources on Theravada Buddhism.
  - **Articles** – written pieces introducing or exploring Theravada topics.
  - **Blogs** – ongoing commentary, reflections, and teachings in blog format.
  - **Books** – recommended reading lists and Theravada-related publications.
  - **Dhamma Talks**
    - **Audio** – recorded talks and lectures on Theravada teachings.
  - **Forums** – online discussion boards and communities.
  - **Organizations** – institutions and groups related to Theravada Buddhism.
  - **Pali** – resources for the Pāli language and canon.
  - **Places**
    - **Monasteries** – Theravada monasteries.
    - **Temples** – Theravada temples.
    - **Hermitages** – retreat and hermitage locations.
    - **Meditation Centers** – practice and retreat centers.
  - **Russian Language** – Theravada materials available in Russian.
  - **Suttas**
    - **Individual Suttas** – links to specific discourses.
    - **Resources** – general sutta collections and tools.
  - **Videos**
    - **Individual Videos** – standalone video teachings.
    - **YouTube Channels** – channels focused on Theravada content.

- **Open Contribution**
  - Public repository with a `CONTRIBUTING.md` file for guidelines on how to propose additions or changes to the list.

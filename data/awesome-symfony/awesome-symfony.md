# Awesome Symfony

A curated, community-driven list of Symfony bundles, utilities, and learning resources for developers working with the Symfony PHP framework.

**Website:** https://github.com/sitepoint-editors/awesome-symfony#readme  
**Category:** Themed Directories  
**Tags:** awesome-lists, PHP, framework

---

## Overview

Awesome Symfony is a GitHub-hosted “awesome list” that organizes high‑quality tools and references from the Symfony ecosystem. It serves as a starting point for discovering bundles, utilities, and documentation relevant to building applications with Symfony.

---

## Features

- **Curated Symfony Ecosystem List**  
  - Focused on the Symfony PHP framework and its ecosystem.  
  - Includes bundles, tools, and learning resources.

- **Structured by Topic / Use Case**  
  The list is organized into multiple topical sections to simplify discovery:
  - **Administration** – Bundles and tools for back-office/admin interfaces.  
  - **Certification** – Materials and links related to Symfony certification.  
  - **Community** – Community resources such as groups, portals, and channels.  
  - **Development** – Developer-oriented tools, helpers, and workflows.  
  - **Distributions** – Symfony distributions and starter kits.  
  - **Ecommerce** – Ecommerce-related bundles and integrations.  
  - **Forms** – Extensions and helpers around Symfony’s Form component.  
  - **Internationalization** – i18n and localization-related packages.  
  - **Miscellaneous** – Useful items that don’t fit other categories.  
  - **Monitoring** – Tools for logging, profiling, and application monitoring.  
  - **Payments Management** – Payment and billing-related integrations.  
  - **Queues** – Asynchronous processing, message queues, and workers.  
  - **Reading** – Articles, guides, and other reading materials.  
  - **Recipes** – Practical how-tos and example configurations.  
  - **Resources** – General learning and reference links for Symfony.  
  - **Service Container** – Tools and extensions around Symfony’s DI container.  
  - **Storage** – Persistence, file storage, and related bundles.  
  - **Template Engine** – Templating tools and integrations (e.g., Twig-related).  
  - **Third Party APIs** – Integrations and clients for external APIs.  
  - **User Management** – Authentication, authorization, and user-related bundles.  
  - **Validation** – Validation libraries and helpers.  
  - **Web Services** – Tools for building or consuming web services and APIs.

- **Open Contribution Model**  
  - Public repository with a dedicated `CONTRIBUTING.md` file.  
  - Community contributions are accepted and curated.

- **Automation & Quality Checks**  
  - Contains a `.travis.yml` configuration, indicating basic automated checks for contributions.

## SourceForge.net Research Data

**Category:** Datasets  
**Provider / Brand:** University of Notre Dame OSS Group  
**Slug:** `sourceforgenet-research-data`

### Overview
SourceForge.net Research Data is a collection of datasets about open‑source software projects and related activity on SourceForge, compiled by the University of Notre Dame OSS Group. It is referenced in the *Awesome Data Project* as a resource for studying open‑source software development as a social network.

These datasets are intended for researchers and analysts interested in software engineering, open‑source ecosystems, collaboration networks, and project activity patterns.

### Features
- **SourceForge project datasets**  
  - Structured data describing projects hosted on SourceForge.  
  - Suitable for large‑scale empirical studies of open‑source projects.

- **Activity and interaction data**  
  - Captures aspects of project and developer activity on SourceForge (e.g., contributions, participation, or other interaction signals, as available in the Notre Dame collection).

- **Social network focus**  
  - Designed and curated specifically to support research on open‑source software development as a social network (e.g., relationships between developers, projects, and contributions).

- **Research‑oriented compilation**  
  - Assembled and maintained by the University of Notre Dame OSS Group for use in academic and empirical research.  
  - Listed in the Awesome Data Project, indicating relevance and recognition within the data/research community.

- **Open data / public research resource**  
  - Treated as an open research dataset for analysis, modeling, and reproducible studies of OSS ecosystems (subject to any conditions set by the original maintainers).

### Typical Use Cases
- Empirical software engineering and OSS ecosystem studies.
- Social network and collaboration analysis among open‑source developers.
- Mining software repositories and project evolution analysis.
- Benchmarking algorithms for network analysis, recommendation, or community detection in OSS contexts.

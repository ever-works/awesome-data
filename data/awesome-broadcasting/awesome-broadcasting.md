# Awesome Broadcasting

**Website:** https://github.com/ebu/awesome-broadcasting#readme  
**Category:** Themed Directories  
**Tags:** media, streaming, awesome-lists

## Overview
Awesome Broadcasting is a curated, open-source “awesome list” of tools, standards, and learning resources related to broadcast technologies and workflows. It focuses on software and specifications useful to broadcasters, streaming platforms, and media engineers.

## Features
- **Curated open-source directory** of broadcast-related tools, libraries, and reference implementations.
- **Broadcast technology focus**, covering end-to-end workflows from production to distribution.
- **Organized by topic**, making it easier to discover tools for specific broadcasting needs.
- **Emphasis on open standards** such as AMWA NMOS and related specifications.
- **Educational resources** section with blogs and websites to build knowledge and skills.
- **Community-driven contributions**, with guidelines for adding and maintaining entries.

### Main Categories Covered
- **Animation, Graphics & Video Playout** – tools for broadcast graphics, playout engines, and related workflows.
- **Clocks & Studio Screens** – studio clocks, tally displays, and production screen tools.
- **Codecs** – software and resources related to audio/video codecs used in broadcast.
- **Communication** – intercoms, messaging, and coordination tools for production teams.
- **Companion Screens** – second-screen and companion app related technologies.
- **Connected TVs** – tools and libraries targeting smart/connected TV platforms.
- **Control Systems** – control and orchestration tools for broadcast equipment and workflows.
- **Distributed Media Processing** – frameworks and tools for processing media across multiple machines or locations.
- **Documentation** – documentation resources and tools tied to broadcasting projects.
- **DVB & WiFi** – resources related to DVB standards and broadcast over WiFi contexts.
- **Hybrid Radio** – tools and standards for combining broadcast and IP-based radio.
- **LiveIP (Audio/Video over IP & Streaming)** – IP-based live production and streaming resources.
- **Media Players** – software media players useful in professional/broadcast environments.
- **Metadata** – tools and specs for handling media and broadcast metadata.
- **Monitoring & Quality Control** – quality assurance, monitoring, and measurement tools.
- **Multimedia Content Processing** – transcoding, packaging, and media manipulation tools.
- **Network & Storage Testing** – testing and benchmarking tools for media networks and storage.
- **NMOS** – Networked Media Open Specifications, including open-source reference implementations.
- **Podcasting** – tools and resources specifically for podcast production and distribution.
- **Radio Production** – software for producing and managing radio content.
- **Rundown Automation** – tools for managing rundowns and newsroom/production automation.
- **SCTE-35** – tools and libraries for ad-insertion and signaling using SCTE-35.
- **Software-defined Radio** – SDR-related tools and frameworks.
- **Streaming** – streaming servers, clients, and related streaming technologies.
- **Subtitling** – captioning and subtitling tools and workflows.
- **Video Production** – non-linear editing, live video tools, and related production software.

### Resource Sections
- **Blogs** – curated blogs focused on broadcast engineering and media technology.
- **Websites** – reference sites, documentation hubs, and knowledge bases for broadcasting.

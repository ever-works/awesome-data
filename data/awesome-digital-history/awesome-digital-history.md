**URL:** https://github.com/maehr/awesome-digital-history#readme  
**Live directory:** https://awesome-digital-history.pages.dev/

**Tags:** history, data-science, research

> Curated “Awesome” list of online primary sources and learning resources for computer-aided, data-driven, and computational approaches to historical research.

---

## Overview

Awesome Digital History is a curated directory of:
- Online textual and multimedia primary sources (with a focus on the Western Hemisphere and the 19th–20th centuries), and
- Courses and learning tools for doing digital / computational history.

It is part of the broader **sindresorhus/awesome** ecosystem and is maintained as an open GitHub repository, with a separate searchable website offering filters and search.

---

## Features

### 1. Scope and Focus
- Emphasis on **primary sources** (textual and multimedia).
- Strong focus on **Western hemisphere**, especially **19th and 20th centuries**.
- Includes **learning materials** for digital and computational history methods.
- Built as an **Awesome list**, following community curation practices.

### 2. Content Organization
The directory is structured into major sections:

- **Archives and primary sources**
  - Categorized geographically and thematically, including:
    - **Africa**
      - *PARC* – Portal for African Research Collections, a meta-catalogue for Africana collections at Basel.
      - *West African Arabic Manuscript Database* – Comprehensive collection of manuscripts documenting Islamic scholarly traditions in West Africa.
    - **Asia**
      - *FOCAL / Asia Directories and Chronicles* – Reference work for foreign traders in Asia, published annually (1863–1941) by the Hong Kong Daily Press.
    - **Austria**
      - *ANNO* – Austrian Newspapers Online.
      - *Digitarium* – Digital edition of the historical **Wiener Zeitung**.
      - *Österreichische Mediathek* – Austrian archive for sound recordings and videos from cultural and contemporary history.
      - *Wien Geschichte Wiki* – Vienna history wiki based on the *Historisches Lexikon Wien* by Felix Czeike, with tens of thousands of contributions and images.
    - **Europe**
      - *Archives Portal Europe* – European archives meta-search engine.
      - *Encyclopédie d'histoire numérique (EHNE)* – European history framed through renewed historiographic questions and contemporary issues.
      - *EuroDocs – Online Sources for European History* – Curated directory of online primary sources, organized by country and period, often hosted by reputable institutions and using Dublin Core metadata.
      - *Europeana* – Search engine for European textual and non-textual archival sources.
      - *handrit.is* – Catalogue of over 15,000 manuscripts (12th–20th c.) in Icelandic, Old Norse, Danish, Latin and more; over 4,000 digitized.
      - *islandskort.is* – Digitized maps of Iceland, Greenland, and the Arctic region (1545–1949).

### 4. Resource Types Covered
- **Newspapers and periodicals** (e.g., ANNO, Digitarium).
- **Manuscripts and rare materials** (e.g., handrit.is, West African Arabic Manuscript Database).
- **Maps and cartographic sources** (e.g., islandskort.is).
- **Audio-visual archives** (e.g., Österreichische Mediathek).
- **Reference works and encyclopedias** (e.g., EHNE, Wien Geschichte Wiki, Asia Directories and Chronicles).
- **Aggregators and meta-catalogues** (e.g., Archives Portal Europe, PARC, Europeana).

### 5. Metadata and Curation Quality
- Many resources are:
  - Hosted by **research institutions**, archives, and libraries.
  - Described using **standardized metadata** (e.g., Dublin Core, as noted for EuroDocs).
- Each entry typically includes:
  - Name and link.
  - Short scope/coverage description.
  - Temporal and geographic focus when known.

### 6. Community and Maintenance
- Hosted on **GitHub** under the Awesome framework.
- Open to **community contributions** via pull requests.
- Uses standard Awesome list conventions (badge, structure, contribution guidelines).

# OpenfMRI

**Category:** Datasets  
**Tags:** datasets, neuroscience, medical-imaging

OpenfMRI is an open database of task-based and resting-state MRI (including fMRI) datasets designed to support data sharing and reuse in cognitive and clinical neuroscience. It provides free access to raw MRI data for the research community.

---

## Key Facts
- **Type:** Open MRI / fMRI dataset repository
- **Data access:** Free and open
- **Current scale (as stated on site):**
  - 95 datasets
  - 3,372 subjects (across all datasets)

---

## Features

### Open MRI Dataset Access
- Provides magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) datasets, including **raw data**.
- Includes **task-based** and **resting-state** fMRI and related MRI data.
- Datasets are **openly available** to the research community.
- Central resource for sharing data in **cognitive** and **clinical neuroscience**.

### Standardized Data Organization (BIDS)
- All datasets conform to the **BIDS (Brain Imaging Data Structure)** organization scheme.
- Consistent **file naming** and structure across all datasets.
- Facilitates easier:
  - Data discovery
  - Analysis workflows
  - Sharing and reproducibility across labs and tools

### Project and Community Resource
- Dedicated to **free and open sharing** of MRI datasets.
- Serves as a **repository** for researchers to make their MRI data available to others.
- Focused on enabling **data reuse** and promoting open science in neuroimaging.

# Awesome Emails

A curated, open-source directory of resources, templates, and tools for designing and developing HTML emails.

**Website:** https://github.com/jonathandion/awesome-emails#readme  
**Category:** Themed Directories  
**Tags:** awesome-lists, web-development, design

---

## Overview

Awesome Emails is a community-maintained “awesome list” focused on helping designers and developers build better HTML emails. It aggregates frameworks, templates, tools, services, learning materials, and design resources into a single, organized reference.

---

## Features

### 1. Frameworks
- Curated list of email-related frameworks.
- Includes React-based email solutions.
- Miscellaneous frameworks for HTML email structure and layout.

### 2. Cheatsheets
- Quick-reference materials for email development.
- Aimed at common HTML email quirks and best practices.

### 3. Templates
- Collection of ready-made HTML email templates.
- Useful for transactional, marketing, and newsletter emails.

### 4. Tools
- Assorted utilities for building and managing email code.
- May include inliners, builders, generators, and workflow helpers.

### 5. Services
- List of hosted services that assist with email creation, sending, or management.

### 6. Self-Hosted Services
- Options for running email-related tooling and infrastructure on your own servers.

### 7. Articles
- Curated reading on email development and design.
- Covers techniques, best practices, and technical deep dives.

### 8. Design Resources
- Guidelines and inspiration for visual email design.
- May include color, typography, layout, and component examples.

### 9. Blogs
- Ongoing publications focused on email design and development.

### 10. General Resources
- Additional references, documentation, and libraries useful for email work.

### 11. Inspirations
- Examples and galleries showcasing effective email designs.

### 12. Statistics
- Data and reports related to email usage, performance, and trends.

### 13. Testing
- Tools and services to test HTML emails across clients and devices.

### 14. Learning
- Educational material for beginners and advanced practitioners.
- May include tutorials, courses, guides, and reference docs.

### 15. Open Contribution
- Public contribution guidelines (`contributing.md`).
- Suggestions and pull requests are explicitly welcomed.

### 16. Licensing
- Licensed under CC0 (public domain dedication), allowing free reuse and adaptation.

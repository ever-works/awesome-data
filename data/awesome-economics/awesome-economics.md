# Awesome Economics

**Description:**  
A curated, periodically updated collection of links for economists, designed as an “Awesome list” starter kit for studying, research, data, software, and careers in economics.

**Website:** https://github.com/antontarasenko/awesome-economics#readme

---

## Features

### Scope and Purpose
- Curated collection of economics resources, organized as part of the broader ["Awesome" lists](https://github.com/sindresorhus/awesome) ecosystem.
- Focused on being a practical starter kit for economists and economics students.
- Periodically updated with new links and resources.

### Main Sections
- **Studying**  
  - **Courses**: Links to economics courses suitable for structured learning.  
  - **Useful Materials**: Supplementary reading, guides, and reference materials for studying economics.  
  - Emphasis on academic resources (e.g. `.edu` sources).

- **Research**  
  - **Portals**: Entry points to large collections of research papers and economics literature.  
  - **Articles and Working Papers**: Guidance on locating papers and working papers (e.g., example search pattern: `site:ideas.repec.org <search term>`).  
  - **Data**:  
    - Datasets for empirical and applied economics.  
    - Search tools and portals for discovering economics datasets.  
  - **Software**:  
    - **Writing**: Tools for drafting, typesetting, and managing research documents.  
    - **Computing**: Software for data analysis, econometrics, and simulations.  
    - **Sharing**: Platforms and tools for sharing code, data, and research outputs.  
    - **Reviews**: Resources reviewing or comparing research and software tools.  
  - **Useful Materials**: Additional research-related references and guidance.

- **Discussions**  
  - Links to discussion forums, communities, and platforms where economics topics are debated and explored.

- **Career**  
  - **Undergraduate**: Resources for undergrad economics students (study advice, applications, opportunities).  
  - **Graduate**: Information and links for graduate-level economics study and research paths.  
  - **Faculty**: Resources relevant to academic economists, including professional development and academic career information.

- **Economics on GitHub** *(sorted alphabetically)*  
  - **Economists**: GitHub profiles of economists who publish code, data, or tools.  
  - **Projects**: Economics-related GitHub repositories, including code, toolkits, and research projects.

- **Links Sent by Readers**  
  - Reader-submitted resources maintained in a dedicated section, allowing community-driven expansion of the list.

### Contribution and Maintenance
- Open to contributions via pull requests following the `contributing.md` guidelines.
- New content can be added directly or emailed to the maintainer.
- Hosted as a public GitHub repository with visible history, contributors, and releases.

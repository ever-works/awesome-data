# Awesome Funny Markov Chains

**Website:** https://github.com/sublimino/awesome-funny-markov#readme  
**Category:** Themed Directories  
**Tags:** fun, machine-learning, awesome-lists

## Overview
Awesome Funny Markov Chains is a facetiously curated list of delightfully amusing Markov chain outputs and related projects. It focuses on humorous, experimental, and playful uses of Markov chains across web, media, and tech contexts.

## Features
- **Curated humor-focused list**
  - Collects examples of funny and facetious Markov chain text and output.
  - Emphasizes entertainment and playfulness over strictly serious applications.

- **Organized by topic** (via table of contents)
  - **Web and Tech** – links to web-based and technical Markov chain experiments.
  - **Twitter** – accounts or bots generating humorous Markov-based tweets.
  - **Media** – Markov chain content involving audio, video, or other media formats.
  - **Tools** – utilities and software for generating or experimenting with Markov text.
  - **A history of funny Markov chains** – references and examples documenting how humorous Markov outputs have evolved.
  - **Sober applications** – more serious or practical uses of Markov chains, presented alongside the humorous ones.

- **Resource hub for Markov chains & machine learning**
  - Provides a pointer to broader learning material via “Awesome Machine Learning” for users wanting more background on Markov chains and related techniques.

- **Open contribution model**
  - Welcomes community contributions through pull requests.
  - Contribution guidelines are provided in a dedicated `contributing.md` file.

- **License & reuse**
  - Published under the **CC0 (Public Domain)** license, allowing free use, copying, and modification of the list content.

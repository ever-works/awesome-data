## Overview

Awesome Credit Modeling is a curated, community-driven list of papers, articles, and other resources focused on credit scoring and credit risk modeling. It aggregates academic and practitioner materials covering statistical and machine learning approaches to assessing creditworthiness and probability of default.

## Features

- **Curated resource collection**
  - Growing list of “awesome” resources on credit scoring and credit risk modeling
  - Emphasis on both theoretical foundations and practical applications

- **Introduction to credit scoring and risk modeling**
  - Links to classic reviews and foundational papers
  - Coverage of statistical classification methods for consumer credit scoring
  - Discussion of how lenders classify applicants into risk classes and estimate probability of default

- **Academic and review papers**
  - *Statistical Classification Methods in Consumer Credit Scoring: A Review* – overview of statistical methods used in credit scoring
  - *Consumer Finance: Challenges for Operational Research* – historical development of credit scoring, definition of credit scores, and ten outlined challenges for operational research in consumer finance

- **Machine learning in credit risk**
  - *Machine Learning in Credit Risk Modeling* whitepaper – overview of machine learning applications in credit risk management and modeling

- **Socio-technical and historical perspectives**
  - *'Lending by numbers': credit scoring and the constitution of risk within American consumer credit* – examination of how statistical methods shape the understanding and construction of credit risk

- **Repository structure and contribution**
  - `resources/` directory organizing the listed materials
  - `CONTRIBUTING.md` describing how to propose new resources or improvements
  - Licensed via a public `LICENSE` file for open use and contributions

## Use Cases

- Discovering key literature on credit scoring and credit risk modeling
- Exploring statistical, OR, and machine learning methods for credit risk
- Studying the evolution and challenges of consumer credit scoring
- Building reading lists or syllabi for courses or self-study in credit risk analytics

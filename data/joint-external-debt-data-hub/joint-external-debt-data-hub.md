# Joint External Debt Data Hub

**Website:** http://www.jedh.org/

## Overview
The Joint External Debt Hub (JEDH) is a joint data portal created by the Bank for International Settlements (BIS), the International Monetary Fund (IMF), the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), and the World Bank (WB). It provides a unified view of external debt statistics and selected foreign assets, integrating information from international creditor/market sources and national debtor sources.

## Features
- **Unified external debt portal**: Central access point for external debt statistics across countries and territories.
- **Jointly developed by four major institutions**:
  - Bank for International Settlements (BIS)
  - International Monetary Fund (IMF)
  - Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD)
  - World Bank Group (WB)
- **Data coverage**:
  - External debt data
  - Selected foreign assets

- **Historical continuation**:
  - Replaces the "Joint BIS-IMF-OECD-WB Statistics on External Debt" website launched in 1999.
  - Continues and expands the role of providing international external debt statistics, originally focused on developing and transition countries and territories.
- **Standardized units**:
  - Data generally provided in millions of US dollars, unless otherwise specified.
- **Sovereign and external debt focus**:
  - Serves as a centralized hub for sovereign and external debt data used in economics and finance research.

## Data & Access
- External debt and related statistics aggregated from multiple official sources (creditor/market and national debtor/QEDS).
- International scope, with emphasis on developing and transition economies but also broader cross-country coverage.

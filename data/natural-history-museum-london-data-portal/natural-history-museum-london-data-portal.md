# Natural History Museum London Data Portal

The Natural History Museum London Data Portal provides open access to millions of specimen, collection, and research records from the Museum’s holdings.

## Overview
- **Type:** Open data portal / dataset catalog
- **Provider:** Natural History Museum, London (Biodiversity Informatics Group)
- **Focus:** Specimens, biodiversity, and research datasets from the Museum’s collections
- **Access:** Web interface, downloads, and API

## Features

### Collections & Coverage
- Access to a large portion of the Museum’s **80 million** objects and specimens.
- Search across:
  - **Specimens:** 6,096,844+ specimen records
  - **Collections:** 6,926,225+ collection records
  - **Everything:** 35,221,487+ total records from collections and research datasets
- Includes natural history data such as zoological, entomological, and other biodiversity-related materials.

### Search & Exploration
- Search interfaces for:
  - **Specimens**
  - **Collections**
  - **All records** (collections + research datasets)
- Dedicated **“Explore the data”** section for browsing collection and research datasets.

### Featured Datasets & Tools
- **Image viewer**
  - Interactive viewer for images in datasets.
  - Zoom capability for high-resolution images.
  - Includes high-resolution photographs of pinned insect drawers.

- **Butterfly types**
  - Access to the Museum’s butterfly type specimens.
  - Provides reference material for Papilionoidea and related digitisation projects.

- **Index lots**
  - Catalogue of index lot records.
  - Used to record the presence of certain entomological and zoological species in the Museum’s collection.

- **Aphid Slide Digitisation**
  - Fully digitised collection of **Aphidoidea slides**.
  - Over 200,000 slides imaged in 2023.
  - Browse and search within this specialised collection.

### Data Access & Reuse
- **Download options**
  - Datasets available for download.
  - Additional information via a dedicated “Download” section.
- **API access**
  - Datasets can be accessed programmatically via an API.

### Citation & DOIs
- Guidance on **how to cite** datasets.
- Dataset-level **DOIs** provided for referencing Museum data in research.

### Technical & Open Source Aspects
- The portal is an **open source project** maintained by the Museum’s Biodiversity Informatics Group.
- Source code is publicly available on **GitHub** (ckanext-nhm).
- Mapping and base tiles provided by **MapQuest** and **OpenStreetMap** contributors.
